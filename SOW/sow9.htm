9. Data Collection from Continuous Water Quality Monitoring Stations in Savannah Estuary System

As part of the Savannah Harbor Expansion Project, the US Army Corps of Engineers, Savannah District committed to install and/or upgrade eight continuous water quality data monitoring stations in the Savannah Harbor estuary. Once installed, the SHEP will operate the gages for one year pre-construction, during construction, and for ten years post-construction. In addition, four of the gages will be maintained for the 50-year life of the project.

The goal of preconstruction monitoring is to establish a baseline set of data to assist with impact assessments during the construction and post construction phases. The goal of monitoring during construction period and post-construction is to ensure that the project features, including flow re-routing in the estuary, are functioning as they were designed.

PERIOD OF PERFORMANCE: Monitoring will take place on all eight stations pre-construction, during construction, and for 10 years after construction. Monitoring will continue after the tenth year on four of the eight stations.
