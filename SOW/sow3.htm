3. Update Hydrodynamic and Water Quality Models

As part of the Savannah Harbor Expansion Project (SHEP), the US Army Corps of Engineers, Savannah District has committed to update the hydrodynamic and water quality models developed for the project if it is decided that an update is warranted. Any model revisions would be reviewed by the natural resource agencies.

Much of this work is linked to construction activities. For these scopes focused on modeling and monitoring the term “construction” applies to construction work taking place in the inner harbor or upper estuary. It does not include any outer harbor construction as those activities are not expected to significantly impact water quality or salinity within the upper estuary.

The goal of these tasks is to develop a tool for describing water quality impacts (salinity and dissolved oxygen) due to SHEP in the Savannah River estuary based on best available information. These tools will enable decision makers in determining project impacts and if necessary any adaptive management strategies.

PERIOD OF PERFORMANCE: Tasks may be initiated as early as February 2013 but are not to be completed prior to the pre-construction data collection effort and intensive environmental sampling events.
